## Menu Philosophy

Dominican food culture reinterpreted as cocktail architecture. Not "Dominican ingredients dropped into classic templates" — Dominican *dishes and traditions* deconstructed into liquid form. Technique-forward but never technique-for-technique's-sake. Every technique serves the flavor.

## The 12-Drink Menu

### Aperitivos y Refrescantes

#### 1. Sueño Clarificado — Milk-Clarified Punch
- **Base**: Pierre Ferrand 1840 Cognac + Ten To One White Rum
- **Modifiers**: Pierre Ferrand Dry Curacao, Licor 43 (BACKLOADED after clarification)
- **Technique**: Milk clarification (evap + whole milk blend), Oleo-Tang, acid-adjusted OJ
- **Key specs (batch)**: 105g orange peels, 75g white sugar, 80g Tang, 4g citric acid, 2g malic acid, 300g OJ, 150g chamomile tea, 420g Cognac, 60g Dry Curacao, 120g Ten To One Rum, 150g whole milk, 150g evap milk → backload 100g Licor 43
- **Acid**: 4g citric + 2g malic (mimics naranja agria). Tang also contains acid.
- **Tea**: Chamomile (NOT cinnamon/allspice — avoids overlap with rest of menu)
- **Status**: TASTE-TESTED. Multiple iterations. Was too tart → fixed by restoring sugar + adding Dry Curacao.
- **Notes**:
  - Punch INTO milk, never reverse
  - Backloading = adding ingredients after clarification that don't contain tannins (preserves volatile aromatics)
  - Triple coffee filter straining
  - Tang is intentional and non-negotiable

#### 2. Chinola Eléctrica — Passion Fruit Highball
- **Base**: Dominican White Rum
- **Modifiers**: Chinola (passion fruit), Cayenne-Agave
- **Technique**: Cayenne-infused agave
- **Status**: Spec'd, not extensively tested
- **Notes**: Simplest drink on the menu. Every menu needs one the bartender can bang out in 30 seconds.

#### 3. Coco Concon Refrescante — Samaná Homage
- **Base**: JW Black Label, Blender Fat-Washed with concon
- **Modifiers**: Velvet Falernum, **Guandules (pigeon pea) Orgeat**, Coconut Water
- **Technique**: Flash-blend fried concon into scotch (10-15s) → freeze → strain
- **Regional homage**: SAMANÁ — coconut + pigeon pea + concon = guandules con coco meets crispy rice
- **Status**: Spec'd. Guandules orgeat needs R&D (same technique as cashew orgeat for Malta Flip).
- **Notes**:
  - NO naranja agria — concon is eaten as standalone salty snack, not sauced
  - Dominicans love Scotch (JW Black, Buchanan's)
  - Concon must be real caldero concon, oven-dried to cracker-dry, fried in neutral oil until deep golden
  - Guandules orgeat replaces High-Saline Demerara (orgeat provides sweetness + body + earthiness)
  - Concon carries salt through fat-wash; add saline drops if needed

#### 4. Mabí Burbujas — Mabí Highball
- **Base**: Rye
- **Modifiers**: Mabí Bark Syrup (push drier — reduce sugar or convert to tincture + minimal demerara), Lime, Dry Tonic
- **Technique**: Mabí bark syrup/tincture
- **Status**: Spec'd, not tested
- **Notes**: Best candidate to become the bone-dry refresher. Reduce syrup sweetness or use bark tincture + barspoon demerara.

### Cócteles con Carácter

#### 5. Negroni Dorado — White Negroni
- **Base**: 1.5oz Plantain-Infused Gin (Fords or Tanqueray No. Ten — NOT Monkey 47)
- **Modifiers**: 1oz Dolin Blanc (or 0.5oz Blanc + 0.5oz Fino Sherry split), 0.75oz Suze
- **Technique**: Fry ripe maduros (DARK — deep amber/mahogany) → cool → infuse in gin 24-48hr → freeze → skim fat cap → coffee filter strain
- **Status**: Spec'd from Gemini R&D. Infusion needs real R&D. NEXT UP.
- **Notes**:
  - No cacao nibs — Suze already does bitter work, nibs would muddy the plantain
  - No default saline in stirred drinks
  - Monkey 47 rejected: too many botanicals get muddied by plantain infusion
  - Sherry split TBD — A/B test with and without
  - Frying dark enough is critical: light golden = banana, deep caramel = plantain
  - Test BOTH pan-fried (Method A) and oven-roast 400°F (Method B) side by side
  - Ratio starting point: 2 medium plantains per 750ml gin
  - Tasting schedule: 24hr, 36hr, 48hr — find the window where plantain is present but gin botanicals aren't buried

#### 6. Mango de Fuego y Humo — Tropical Sour
- **Base**: Smith & Cross Rum + Mezcal split
- **Modifiers**: Smoked Mango Honey, Lime, Egg White
- **Technique**: Smoked mango honey
- **Possible enhancer**: Green Chartreuse (0.25-0.5oz) — use with caution, lots of voices already
- **Status**: Spec'd, not tested

#### 7. Cafecito con Espuma Salada — Coffee Aperitivo
- **Base**: Citrus-Forward Gin (Hendrick's, Roku, or Tanq 10)
- **Modifiers**: Cap Corse Blanc (Corsican quinquina — this is the dominant flavor, not the gin), Light-Roast Cometeer espresso, Shaken Pineapple Juice (creates natural froth), Salt sprinkle on top
- **Technique**: Shaken pineapple juice creates cafecito-style froth, salt mimics salted foam coffee
- **Status**: TASTE-TESTED. Jeriel loved it. Cap Corse Blanc dominates, gin recedes. Pineapple froth + salt = salted foam cafecito texture.
- **Notes**: Originally flagged as "riskiest drink" — actually proven and delicious.

#### 8. Ensalada Rusa Roja — Savory Stirred
- **Base**: Roasted Beet-Infused Potato Vodka + Aquavit split
- **Modifiers**: Fino Sherry, Escabeche Brine
- **Technique**: Roasted beet infusion
- **Status**: Spec'd, not tested
- **Notes**: Aquavit adds caraway/dill savory spice. Driest non-stirred drink on menu (sweetness: 1/10).

### Tragos Fuertes y Ricos

#### 9. Esencia de Habichuela Dulce — Served VISCOUS on a Rock
- **Base**: Aged Dominican Rum + Blackstrap Rum
- **Modifiers**: Spiced Bean & Batata Nectar, Rum-Raisin
- **Technique**: Pectinex + agar-agar for viscosity (NOT clarified — served thick/rich)
- **Status**: Spec'd, not tested
- **Notes**: Differentiated from Piña Colada by texture — this is viscous/rich, Piña Colada is crystal clear/elegant.

#### 10. El Tabaquero Francés — Tenares Homage (Smoked Old Fashioned)
- **Base**: VSOP Cognac
- **Modifiers**: Dulce de Leche & **Cacao Husk Tea** Syrup (reduced dulce de leche by ~25%), Cynar (0.25oz), Angostura, Tobacco Smoke
- **Technique**: Cacao husk tea brewed from Tenares cacao shells; stirred; smoked glass
- **Regional homage**: TENARES — tobacco + cacao from the same valley. Two agricultural products from the same land.
- **Status**: Spec'd. Cynar addition and cacao husk tea swap need R&D.
- **Notes**:
  - Cacao husk tea is light/fruity/floral, NOT heavy chocolate — it's the terroir of cacao before processing
  - Cynar pushes drink from sweet toward bitter-sweet (breaks the rich/sweet cluster)
  - Reduced dulce de leche syrup compensates for Cynar addition
  - Possible Cynar enhancer already discussed — now committed

#### 11. Alma de Piña Colada — Crystal Clear "Ghost" — Served UP
- **Base**: Toasted Coconut-Infused White Rum (infusion, not fat-wash)
- **Modifiers**: Pineapple Oleo, Lime Bitters, Allspice
- **Technique**: Toasted coconut infusion, pineapple oleo-saccharum
- **Possible enhancer**: Yellow Chartreuse
- **Status**: Spec'd, not tested
- **Notes**: Differentiated from Habichuela Dulce by being clarified/elegant vs viscous/rich. Served up.

#### 12. Malta de Cajuil Flip — Egg Flip
- **Base**: Bourbon
- **Modifiers**: Malta, Cashew Orgeat ("Dulce de Cajuil"), Whole Egg, Nutmeg
- **Technique**: Cashew orgeat, dry shake + wet shake
- **Status**: Spec'd, not tested

## Menu Constraints

- Only 1 milk-clarified drink (Sueño) — keeps it special
- Only 1 flip (Malta) — whole egg is a commitment
- Only 1 fat-wash (Coco Concon) — rest are infusions
- Max 3 heavy cocktails — balance with refreshing/aperitivo drinks
- Limit baking spice overlap across menu (why Sueño uses chamomile, not cinnamon)
- Tang is non-negotiable in Sueño
- No default saline in stirred drinks
- No centrifuge — enzyme clarification (Pectinex) and coffee filters only
- Oleo-saccharum: use PEELS not zest (zest doesn't get liquidy enough)
- Syrup making: vigorous shaking in hot water, no boiling

## Regional Homages

### Samaná (Jeriel's family)
- **Drink**: Coco Concon Refrescante (#3)
- **Connection**: Coconut + pigeon pea (guandules) + concon = guandules con coco meets crispy rice
- **Key ingredient**: Guandules orgeat replaces demerara syrup
- **About Samaná**: Coconut capital of DR. Peninsula covered in coconut groves. Unique Afro-American community — descendants of freed Black Americans who emigrated in 1824. Over 80% of population descends from these settlers. English-speaking heritage, AME churches. Signature dish: guandules con coco.

### Tenares (Jeriel's family, Cibao valley)
- **Drink**: El Tabaquero Francés (#10)
- **Connection**: Tobacco smoke + cacao husk tea — two agricultural products from the same valley
- **Key ingredient**: Cacao husk tea in the dulce de leche syrup
- **About Tenares**: Agricultural heartland. Grows cacao, coffee, tobacco, banana, cassava. "Chocolate Jungle" cacao farm experiences. Hermanas Mirabal province. Cibao cuisine: hearty, bold, meat-forward.

## Technique Reference

| Technique | Used In | Key Notes |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| Milk clarification + backloading | Sueño | Punch INTO milk. Backload anything without tannins. Triple coffee filter. |
| Oleo-Tang / Oleocitrate | Sueño | PEELS not zest. Citric acid + Tang as abrasive. |
| Blender fat-wash | Coco Concon | Flash blend 10-15s, freeze, skim fat cap, strain. |
| Enzyme clarification (Pectinex) | Alpine cordial, Cafecito foam | Decant + coffee filter polish. |
| Direct warm infusion | Negroni (plantain in gin) | 24-48hr room temp, freeze, strain. |
| Oven roast infusion | Negroni (alternative) | 400°F, 20-25 min, no oil needed. |
| Acid adjustment | Sueño (naranja agria mimic) | Citric + malic acid blend. |
| Spice tea | Coquito | Toast whole spices → smash → steep. Never ground spice directly. |
| Fat-wash spirits | Coquito (coconut oil) | 4oz oil per 750ml. Both spirits washed together. |
| Guandules orgeat | Coco Concon | Same technique as cashew orgeat: soak → toast → blend → strain → sweeten. |
| Cacao husk tea | Tabaquero | Brew shells, not beans. Light/fruity/floral, not chocolate. |

## Expansion Concepts (Not on menu — future exploration)

### Mamajuana Spritz
- Mamajuana base (rum + red wine + honey + bark/herbs) + cava + orange peel
- Could fill the bone-dry refresher gap
- THE most Dominican spirit tradition — currently absent from menu

### Sofrito Aromatics
- Aji gustoso-infused spirit + cilantro element
- Biggest flavor gap: the aromatic herbs/peppers that define Dominican cooking (sofrito base) are nowhere on the menu
- Could be a finishing oil (like Perla Negra's cilantro oil on platano daiquiri) rather than a whole drink

### Tres Golpes (Dominican Breakfast)
- Green plantain-infused aged rum + longaniza fat-wash + saline
- Most aggressive/polarizing concept
- Mangu + queso frito + salami = savory/fatty/salty

### Finishing Oils & Techniques (from Casarré / Perla Negra)
- Pineapple oil, annatto oil, cilantro oil as aromatic finishing layers
- Condensed milk foam as texture element
- These add Dominican identity to the experience without restructuring recipes
